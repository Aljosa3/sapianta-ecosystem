# Canonical Authority Model V1

Status: authority language consolidation only.

## Canonical Definition

Authority is explicit permission to affect a bounded domain.

Authority must be:

- explicit
- scoped
- replay-visible
- constitutionally governed
- non-transferable by implication
- fail-closed on ambiguity

## Authority Families

### Governance Authority

Governance authority defines constitutional boundaries, admissibility, evidence interpretation, and mutation constraints.

Governance authority is not delegated to LLMs, orchestration concepts, runtime sessions, replay artifacts, or operational evidence.

### Runtime Authority

Runtime authority is permission to perform bounded operational actions.

Runtime authority must not emerge from contribution text, orchestration requests, replay visibility, lifecycle states, or identity continuity.

### Orchestration Authority

Orchestration authority is not activated in the current substrate.

Orchestration boundary and lifecycle artifacts model coordination concepts only. They do not authorize dispatch, workers, agents, execution coordination, recursion, or hidden continuation.

### Non-Authoritative Participation

Non-authoritative participation means an actor, model, artifact, or contribution may provide bounded input without gaining permission to execute, mutate, coordinate, persist, or govern.

LLM participation is non-authoritative.

### Escalation Semantics

Escalation is any attempted movement from contribution, visibility, lifecycle state, or replay evidence into authority.

Escalation must fail closed unless explicitly authorized by a future constitutional gate.

## Canonical Rule

Visibility is not authority.

Contribution is not authority.

Lifecycle state is not authority.

Replay evidence is not authority.

Authorization is not execution unless an explicit governed runtime boundary says so.
