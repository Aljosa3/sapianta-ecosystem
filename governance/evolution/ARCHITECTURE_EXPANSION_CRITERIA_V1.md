# ARCHITECTURE_EXPANSION_CRITERIA_V1
Status: CONSTITUTIONAL ANTI-OVERENGINEERING FOUNDATION
Layer: Constitutional Evolution Governance
Principle: Expansion Requires Evidence

---

# 1. PURPOSE

This artifact defines when architectural expansion MAY become justified
inside SAPIANTA/AiGOL.

It does not authorize expansion. It defines constitutional criteria that
must be satisfied before later governed milestones may consider
expansion.

---

# 2. REQUIRED CONDITIONS

Architectural expansion requires:

- demonstrated refinement insufficiency;
- deterministic replay evidence;
- bounded rollback strategy;
- constitutional compatibility;
- operational stability proof;
- governance review;
- operator-visible value;
- clear non-goals;
- known lineage impact;
- known replay impact.

---

# 3. REQUIRED JUSTIFICATION

Any expansion proposal MUST explain:

- why refinement is insufficient;
- why existing substrate cannot support the requirement;
- why complexity increase is necessary;
- why replay impact is acceptable;
- why governance impact is acceptable;
- how rollback remains possible;
- how operator understanding remains preserved;
- how authority boundaries remain unchanged unless explicitly approved.

---

# 4. BLOCKING CONDITIONS

Expansion MUST be blocked if it:

- duplicates governance primitives;
- fragments replay architecture;
- creates hidden authority pathways;
- bypasses approval;
- weakens fail-closed behavior;
- introduces uncontrolled orchestration;
- introduces autonomous mutation authority;
- undermines `OPERATIONAL_BOUNDARY_AND_FREEZE_V1`;
- lacks deterministic evidence.

---

# 5. PROMOTION RELATIONSHIP

Architectural expansion proposals must remain compatible with promotion
governance. Promotion eligibility does not equal execution authority.
Replay verification does not equal promotion authorization.

---

# 6. NON-ACTIVATION

This artifact creates no runtime expansion authority, no adaptive
architecture mutation, no orchestration engine, and no governance
enforcement runtime.
