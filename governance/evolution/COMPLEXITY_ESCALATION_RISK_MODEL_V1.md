# COMPLEXITY_ESCALATION_RISK_MODEL_V1
Status: CONSTITUTIONAL ANTI-OVERENGINEERING FOUNDATION
Layer: Constitutional Evolution Governance
Principle: Complexity Growth Is a Governance Risk

---

# 1. PURPOSE

This artifact defines architectural risk patterns associated with
unbounded complexity escalation.

---

# 2. RISK PATTERNS

Complexity escalation risks include:

- orchestration inflation;
- worker proliferation;
- recursive subsystem layering;
- governance fragmentation;
- replay fragmentation;
- abstraction drift;
- adaptive complexity explosion;
- hidden authority pathways;
- subsystem multiplication without operational evidence;
- framework introduction without demonstrated necessity.

---

# 3. GOVERNANCE IMPACT

Unbounded complexity can weaken:

- replay confidence;
- lineage visibility;
- operator understanding;
- fail-closed clarity;
- rollback traceability;
- promotion discipline;
- constitutional boundary enforcement;
- enterprise-readable audit continuity.

---

# 4. RISK RESPONSE

When complexity risk appears, the preferred response is:

- pause expansion;
- identify the operational pain;
- attempt bounded semantic refinement;
- preserve replay continuity;
- preserve governance visibility;
- require deterministic evidence before expansion.

---

# 5. ESCALATION RULE

Complexity growth without bounded justification is architectural risk.

If justification is uncertain, fail closed at the governance proposal
level and require review before expansion.

---

# 6. NON-ACTIVATION

This risk model is documentation and evidence only. It does not score
architecture at runtime, prune systems, enforce restrictions, or activate
autonomous architectural mutation.
