# 3. HOW TO INTERPRET REPLAY EVIDENCE

Replay evidence should be interpreted as operational traceability.

Replay continuity records whether replay identities remain unique,
ordered, and deterministic.

Replay lineage records how evidence, operations, proposals, promotion
decisions, or rollback events relate over time.

Replay integrity records whether evidence is complete, append-only,
verifiable, and free from ambiguity such as gaps, duplicate identities,
or ordering corruption.

Fail-closed semantics mean that ambiguity, malformed evidence,
corrupted lineage, or unverifiable continuity must be treated as failure
rather than partial acceptance.

Stabilization discoveries should be read as evidence that real operation
surfaced semantic issues. They are not proof of architecture failure and
not permission to introduce new runtime layers.

Replay evidence is operational traceability, not adaptive reasoning.
