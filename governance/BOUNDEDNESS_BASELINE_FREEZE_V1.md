# Boundedness Baseline Freeze V1

Status: boundedness baseline freeze.

## Frozen Boundedness Definition

Boundedness is the constitutional constraint that limits scope, authority, persistence, lifecycle progression, and interpretation.

Boundedness prevents capability drift.

## Frozen Boundedness Terms

The following boundedness terms are frozen as canonical:

- bounded
- fail-closed
- isolation
- containment
- termination
- non-persistence

## Frozen Boundedness Guarantees

Boundedness guarantees:

- explicit scope
- replay visibility
- authority separation
- fail-closed ambiguity handling
- no hidden continuation
- explicit termination where lifecycle is present
- no hidden persistence

## Frozen Fail-Closed Rule

Ambiguity must stop the boundary.

Fail-closed behavior does not permit:

- autonomous retry
- hidden recovery
- inferred continuation
- semantic repair
- governance mutation
- authority escalation

## Prohibited Boundedness Drift

Future evolution must not redefine boundedness as convenience, capability growth, adaptive recovery, orchestration expansion, or persistent cognition.
