# Canonical Replay Language V1

Status: replay vocabulary canonicalization only.

## Canonical Terms

### Replay

Replay is the deterministic evidence substrate that preserves operational and governance-visible history.

Replay is not runtime memory, cognition, orchestration, autonomous interpretation, or hidden persistence.

### Lineage

Lineage is the ordered ancestry of replay-visible artifacts, transitions, sessions, identities, contributions, and governance dispositions.

Lineage must be deterministic, append-only, and fail-closed on ambiguity.

### Continuity

Continuity is preservation of lineage meaning across time without hidden mutation.

Continuity does not mean persistence of autonomous cognition or hidden runtime state.

### Replay Visibility

Replay visibility means an event, transition, contribution, disposition, or termination is explicitly represented as evidence.

Replay visibility is evidence, not authority.

### Replay Integrity

Replay integrity means replay evidence remains ordered, unique, append-only, and unambiguous.

Replay integrity fails closed on gaps, duplication, ordering corruption, lineage ambiguity, or mutation pressure.

### Replay Isolation

Replay isolation means one replay scope cannot contaminate another replay scope.

Replay isolation prevents cross-session mutation, hidden identity carryover, and orchestration resurrection.

## Canonical Rule

All continuity concepts must subordinate themselves to replay-visible lineage.

Semantic continuity, identity continuity, LLM contribution lineage, and orchestration lifecycle progression are domain-specific interpretations of replay visibility, not parallel replay systems.

## Alias Guidance

Terms such as trace, history, ancestry, record, evidence chain, and continuity chain should be treated as aliases only when they preserve the canonical replay meaning.

If an alias implies hidden memory, adaptive reconstruction, semantic inference, or autonomous continuation, it is inadmissible.
