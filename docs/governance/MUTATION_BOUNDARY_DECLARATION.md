# Mutation Boundary Declaration

Status: constitutional governance finalization artifact.

## Immutable Mutation Surfaces

Immutable surfaces include:

- Layer 0 constitutional semantics;
- Layer 1 canonical artifact definitions;
- replay-critical evidence;
- finalized envelopes;
- ledger history;
- finalized governance evidence;
- canonical invariant semantics;
- finalized enforcement hierarchy semantics.

These surfaces must not be mutated by autonomous systems or ordinary development flow.

## Controlled Mutation Surfaces

Controlled surfaces include:

- enforcement implementations;
- conformance rule expansion;
- promotion gate implementation;
- certification reporting;
- hook installation and validation;
- governance evidence packaging;
- lineage indexing.

Controlled mutation requires governance review, test evidence, and preservation of replay-safe evidence.

## Adaptive Mutation Surfaces

Adaptive surfaces include:

- bounded research systems;
- CAL-generated development tasks;
- future remediation proposal layers;
- future governance hardening proposals;
- productization and presentation surfaces outside protected governance paths.

Adaptive mutation may propose or explore, but it cannot bypass higher authority layers.

## Prohibited Mutation Classes

Prohibited mutation classes include:

- hidden mutation of Layer 0 or Layer 1;
- replay-breaking mutation;
- silent activation of dormant governance memory;
- broker/API execution introduction through governance paths;
- autonomous constitutional mutation;
- certification bypass;
- mutation of finalized evidence to hide drift.

## Approval-Required Mutation Classes

Approval is required for:

- structural governance changes;
- restricted Layer 2 changes;
- governance system changes that affect enforcement semantics;
- trusted scope changes;
- hook enforcement changes;
- replay-critical implementation changes;
- changes that alter certification dependencies.

Approval evidence must remain explicit. Missing approval must not be interpreted as approval.
