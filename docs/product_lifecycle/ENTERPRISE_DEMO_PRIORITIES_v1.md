# SAPIANTA Enterprise Demo Priorities v1

## Document Role

This document defines the next Product 1 enterprise demo priorities.

This is productization guidance only. It does not activate runtime governance, change deterministic semantics, or redesign the ecosystem foundation.

## Product 1

Product 1 is the AI Decision Validator.

The demo should make SAPIANTA understandable as an enterprise execution governance layer for AI decisions, with strong audit, replay, explainability, and unknown-decision governance narratives.

## Priority 1: Enterprise Demo UX

Focus:
- Enterprise Demo UX
- cinematic validation flow
- EU AI Act hero messaging
- audit center
- replay visualization

Goal:

Make the first Product 1 demo feel credible, legible, and memorable for enterprise evaluators. The demo should show a decision entering the validator, being evaluated, producing audit evidence, and becoming replayable evidence.

## Priority 2: EU AI Act Execution Governance Positioning

Focus:
- EU AI Act execution governance positioning
- operational risk framing
- auditability
- compliance evidence

Goal:

Position SAPIANTA as an execution governance and evidence layer for AI decision systems. The demo should frame governance as operational evidence, not just policy text or static documentation.

This positioning must avoid implying automatic legal certification or production enforcement.

## Priority 3: Unknown-Decision Governance UX

Focus:
- Unknown-decision governance UX
- governance extension proposal
- human approval
- dormant validation activation

Goal:

Show what happens when governance coverage is incomplete. The UX should clearly represent coverage unknown, governance gap detected, proposal generation, human approval required, dormant validation state, and activation review.

Unknown-decision handling should strengthen trust by showing that uncertain decisions are not silently accepted.

## Priority 4: Stable Demo Release Discipline

Focus:
- stable demo release discipline
- production/demo separation

Goal:

Keep the Hetzner demo server stable and controlled. Product work should happen locally, be committed and pushed to GitHub after validation, and reach the server only through a deliberate demo release decision.

The demo runtime must remain separate from any future production runtime.
